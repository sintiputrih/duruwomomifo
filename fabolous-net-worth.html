Fabolous: Career

Beginnings of musical Career

John David Jackson, aka Fabolous, was a broke man and started to rap in several clubs in New York to earn for living.

In addition, he also recorded and posted his rap records in several online sources like his Facebook, YouTube.

An American artist Fabolous started his singing career from a radio show.

Further, he rapped on the New York City radio station Hot 97.0, in which Fabolous got an invitation by executive record producer DJ Clue.

After a while, the Rap artist Fabolous was taken under the wing by the DJ Clue’s record label called Desert Strom Records.

Soon after signing a deal with the record label, he performed in several mixtapes of DJ Clue as well as in Roc-A-Fella’s mixtapes.

As a result, he was signed by one of the famous record labels, Elektra Records, on September 6, 2001.

Studio Album: Ghetto Fabolous and Street Dreams

The day finally arrived when Fabolous released his debut studio album on September 11, 2001, titled Ghetto Fabolous.

Subsequently, this studio album garnered total sales of 1 million copies worldwide and about 150,000 record sales on the first week.

Further, Ghetto Fabolous topped numerous rap charts, including Billboard 200, with its debut on the fourth spot.

Moving on, Fabolous released yet another studio album called Street Dreams on March 4, 2003, following his successful debut studio album.

Not to mention, this album was labeled platinum by RIAA and gold by BPI, which peaked number 4 on Billboard Hot 100 chart.

After the successful launch of this album, the American rapper released More Street Dreams, Pt. 2: The Mixtape on November 4, 2003

To conclude, all of these albums and mixtape was released in association with Desert Strom Records and Elektra Records.

Albums: Real Talk and From Nothin’ to Somethin’

Following his successful two debut studio albums, Fabolous dropped his third studio album, Real Talk.

Further, this album sold over 100,000 copies in its first week after being released on November 5, 2004.

Meanwhile, the rapper was nominated for the Grammy Award in 2004 for his studio album More Street Dreams.

In addition, Fabolous launched his fourth studio album entitled From Nothin’ to Somethin’ on Def Jam Recordings.

Moreover, the album was launched on June 12, 2007, and sold over 150,000 copies in its first few weeks.

On top of that, the American rap artist finally secured the top spot on both Billboard’s Top R&B/Hip-Hop Albums and Top Rap Albums charts.

As a matter of fact, his third track from this album, entitled Make Me Better, was the one that shoots him to stars.

Not to mention, it featured the fellow artist and one of the greatest singers, Shaffer Chimere Smith, well known by stage name Ne-Yo.

Furthermore, the Make Me Better track was stood at the top of the Hot Rap Track Billboard Chart for 14 weeks in a row.

To sum up, this track was nominated and has won Fabolous few notable awards, including Top Rap Song in 2008 ASCAP Rhythm and Soul Music Awards.

Other Studio Albums

American rap artist Fabolous then went on to release series of studio albums after the year 2008.

Firstly, he released his fifth studio album Loso’s Way, in 2009.

Subsequently, in the same year, Loso’s Way: The Movie was released, and in 2012, Loso’s Way 2: The Movie.

In addition, his sixth studio album, The Young OG Project, was released on Christmas day of 2014.

Finally, after five years of hiatus, the veteran rapper Fabolous dropped a studio album entitled Summertime Shootout 3: Coldest Summer Ever on November 29, 2019.

Along the way, he released a collaborative album called Friday on Elm Street with singer Jason Terrance Phillips aka Jadakiss, on November 24, 2017.
