International Career

On September 23, 2016, he made his Twenty20 International (T20I) debut for the West Indies against Pakistan.

He was picked to the West Indies squad for their tour of India in November 2018. Likewise, he struck an unbroken 53 runs off just 25 balls in the third T20I encounter, his first half-century in T20I cricket.

He was included in the West Indies One Day International (ODI) squad for the series against England in February 2019. On February 20, 2019, he made his ODI debut for the West Indies against England.

He was picked to the West Indies squad for the 2019 Cricket World Cup in April 2019. Pooran earned his first ODI century in a match against Sri Lanka on July 1, 2019.

With 367 runs in nine matches, he finished the tournament as the West Indies’ best run-scorer. Pooran was awarded the team’s Rising Star by the International Cricket Council (ICC) following the World Cup.

For the first time, Cricket West Indies awarded him a central contract in July 2019, ahead of the 2019–20 season.

In place of the original 2020 international captain Kieron Pollard, who injured his hamstring during training, he became vice-captain for the West Indies in T20 international cricket.

He even captained the West Indies cricket team in a 5-match 20-20 international series against Australia, in which the Caribbean side won 4 games to 1.
