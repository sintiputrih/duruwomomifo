Julie Actress

Julie became involved in local theater by 1989 and appeared in the play Street Law. Her first film role was a small speaking part in The Black Cat segment of the Dario Argento/George A. Romero horror film Two Evil Eyes (1990). A year later, she appeared in the TV show Hi Honey, I’m Home! (1991), which was postponed after two seasons.

After graduating from New York University in 1993,  she relocated to Los Angeles. Two weeks after her move, she received a bit part in a 1994 episode of Married… with Children, playing a girl who wanted to lose her virginity to Bud Bundy. Her subsequent roles included an unaired Aaron Spelling television pilot Crosstown Traffic. In 1995 she starred in another unaired TV pilot called Empire

Julie auditioned for the role of Buffy Summers for the television series Buffy the Vampire Slayer in 1996 but lost the role to Sarah Michelle Gellar. However, she was offered the small role of the vampire Darla, in the pilot episode. She had an ongoing role as Joplin Russell in the TV show Ask Harriet in 1998, then guest-starred on both Conrad Bloom and The King of Queens. She made two films, the dark comedy Jawbreaker and Dirt Merchant, before landing a lead role in the TV series Payne, an American remake of the British sitcom Fawlty Towers. It was canceled after eight episodes had aired

In 2005 Julie played the role of Danielle in the direct-to-DVD film Bad Girls from Valley High. She had a small role in the critically acclaimed television film Lackawanna Blues. She was also cast in the straight-to-DVD film 8mm 2 as Lynn. Julie was cast in episodes of Supernatural, CSI: Miami, Law & Order, and CSI: Crime Scene Investigation and had a supporting role in the Swedish independent film Kill Your Darlings (2006),

Julie was cast on The Soup with Joel McHale. Benz next took an ongoing role on Desperate Housewives as Robin Gallagher, a stripper with a heart of gold and a master’s degree in education who gets offered the chance to transition into a more legitimate career. In early 2010, ABC reported that Julie had landed a lead role as Stephanie Powell in the network’s drama pilot No Ordinary Family. She appeared in the new CBS TV show A Gifted Man in 2011. Originally a co-star, she was later downgraded to a recurring role.

Julie landed an ongoing role in the CBS series Hawaii Five-O, in 2015 playing the latest team member and Chin Ho’s girlfriend, Abby Dunn. She also starred in the television film Charming Christmas. She starred in the acclaimed tv series Love, Victor in 2021
