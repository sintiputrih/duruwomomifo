Peter Gerety Biography

Peter Gerety is an American veteran stage, screen and television actor. He is famously known for his role in the NBC police drama Homicide: Life on the Street. He is also known for his appearance on the 2011 NBC series Prime Suspect as Maria Bello’s father.

Born in Providence, Rhode Island, Peter Gerety began acting while he was a student at Boston University. He participated in productions at the Charles Playhouse. In 1965, he joined the Trinity Square Repertory Company. It was a resident theater company in Providence. While there he appeared in over 125 productions.

In early 1992, he performed to critical acclaim on Broadway in Conversations with My Father. He starred as Judd Hirsch, and in Harold Pinter’s Hothouse. Since then, he has performed in many more plays. He has performed on broadway and off-Broadway.

Most recently Peter Gerety has starred in Martin McDonagh’s The Lieutenant of Inishmore. Towards the end of 1990s, he joined the cast of the Barry Levinson produced NBC police drama Homicide: Life on the Street. He also appeared as a regular guest star on Law & Order and HBO’s The Wire. He played FBI Agt. Franklin Morgan in the short-lived American adaptation of Life on Mars.

In the final season of Brotherhood as Martin Kilpatrick. Peter Gerety appeared more recently on Mercy and Brothers and Sisters. He appears in the 2011 NBC series Prime Suspect as Maria Bello’s father. He also appeared in a number of films. Among them include; K-Pax, People I Know and The Curse of the Jade Scorpion.

He has also appeared in Hollywood Ending, Wolf, Charlie Wilson’s War, Surviving Picasso, War of the Worlds, Things That Hang from Trees, Inside Man, and Changeling. He played Louis Piquett in Public Enemies. In 2013, he played the role of John Cotter in Nora Ephron’s Broadway play Lucky Guy with Tom Hanks.

In 2017, Peter Gerety played Otto Bernhardt, the patriarch of the new Amazon series Sneaky Pete.
