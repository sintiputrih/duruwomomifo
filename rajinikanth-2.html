Biography/Wiki

Superstar Rajinikanth was born on 12 December 1950 as Shivaji Rao Gaekwad in Bengaluru, Mysore State (now, in Karnataka), India. The 70-years-old (as of 2020) actor has been one of the most paid and successful actors of the B-town. Rajnikanth is a multitalented actor, producer, screenplay writer, and a philanthropist. He has a struggle story of his own. His life boarded a flight from being a ticket collector in Bangalore Transport Service to an actor with a huge mass popularity. Maratha by blood and having roots in Tamil Nadu, Rajinikanth has worked in Kannada, Tamil, Telugu, Hindi, English, Malayalam, and Bengali films. His contribution to Bollywood has taken the film industry to a next level. He is a very humble, simple, and down to earth person. 

Physical Appearance

Rajinikanth has a charismatic personality. He is 5’8 tall and weighs around 75 Kgs. The actor has Dark Brown eyes and is semi-bald with Grey hair. 

Family, Affair, Wife & Religion

Rajinikanth was born into a Kshatriya Maratha Family but his ancestors were also Tamilians. His father, Ramojirao Gaekwad was a police constable and his mother, Jijabai was a homemaker.

Rajinikanth’s parents

He was a very naughty kid in his childhood. When he was just 8-years-old, his mother passed away, hence, Rajini was raised by his elder brothers and his father.

Career

Rajinikanth did his schooling from Acharya Paathshala in Basavanagudi, Bangalore and Vivekananda Balaka Sangha school. Before he became an actor, he did all sorts of jobs as that of a carpenter, a coolie, to earn a livelihood. He became a bus conductor at Bangalore Transport Service. With the fund aid provided by a friend, Raj Bahadur, Rajinikanth joined the Madras Film Institute. When he was performing at a stage play at Madras Film Institute, Rajini met the director, K Balachander, who offered him a role in his Tamil film.

Rajinikanth with K Balachander

Rajini was not fluent in Tamil but in no time, he mastered the language. In 1975, Rajinikanth made his debut Tamil film, ‘Apoorva Raagangal‘ alongside the actor, Kamal Haasan.

Rajinikanth with Kamal Haasan in Apoorva Raagangal

He was only recognized for negative roles in the first two years of his career until in 1977, he played the pivotal role in the Telugu film, ‘Chilakamma Cheppindi.‘ In 1980, he did the film, Billa (a remake of Amitabh Bachchan’s ‘Don, 1978) which went on to become his first commercial success.

Rajinikanth’s film, Billa

He played the role of a Hindu saint, ‘Raghavendra Swami’ in the film, ‘Sri Raghavendra‘ which was his 100th film. In 1988, Rajinikanth did an Indian-American action-adventure film, ‘Bloodstone.’

Rajinikanth in the film, Bloodstone

His film, ‘Raaja Chinna Roja‘ (1989) became the first animated Tamil film.

Rajinikanth in Raaja Chinna Roja

‘Thalapathi‘ (1991) was his only film that was released with a U/A certificate. Rajinikanth starring film, ‘Enthiran (a science fiction),’ was first offered to Kamal Hassan. In 2012, he was titled, the Most Influential Indian by Forbes India. In 2011, he made a cameo appearance in Shah Rukh Khan starrer film, ‘Ra.One.‘ Meena is the only actress who has worked with him as a child actor and as his heroine.

Rajinikanth with Meena

Controversies

  • In 2014, for his Bollywood film, ‘Main Hoon Rajinikanth,’ he received a stay order from Madras High Court, after which the name of his film was changed to ‘Main Hoon Part-Time Killer.’
  • In 2015, a legal notice was sent to Rajinikanth from Madras High Court on a plea from a financier to take an action against the director, Kasthuri Raja, who is the father of actor Dhanush and a relative of Rajinikanth. Since Kasthuri Raja took the money from the financier using Rajini’s name without his consent, the financier wanted justice through Rajinikanth’s side.

    Kasthuri Raja with Dhanush

  • Pro-Tamils protested against Rajinikanth when in 2017, Lyca Productions announced that a housing scheme would be unveiled by Rajinikanth under his charity wing, ‘Gnanam Foundation‘ for displaced Tamils in Jaffna, Sri Lanka.
